Mechanism

How it works.

  1. G1 Direct Competitor Scan

    Who is the closest existing thing, what have they shipped, where are they weak?

  2. G2 Market Structure

    Is the market shaped so this idea can win meaningful share?

  3. G3 Platform Risk

    Will a platform shift or hyperscaler ship this in 24 months?

  4. G4 Willingness to Pay

    Will the target customer actually pay enough to make this a business?

  5. G5 Why Now

    What changed in the last 24 months that makes this possible now?

Evidence

Real output.

A real validation run on a B2C focus app. Five gates fired; one passed, three failed, one came back inconclusive. Below: the cross-gate snapshot, then the killshot gate (Pricing) in full — sources, tiers, and verdict exactly as the framework rendered them.

G1 Direct Competitor Scan FAIL

⚠ Model judgmentAt least 3 directly-overlapping AI-screen-monitoring products are shipping today — the founder doesn't enter as a first-mover. The "I have the problem too" angle doesn't produce structural differentiation.

G2 Market Demand PASS

⚠ Model judgmentDemand for "focus" tools is real and large, but the only profitable comparable surfaced (Rize, $10k+ MRR) is a time-tracker, not an AI-screen-monitoring app — the demand may be for a different shape of product.

G3 Platform & Big-Tech Risk FAIL

⚠ Model judgmentApple owns the Screen Time / Managed Settings API the product must call, and the same App Review Guidelines surface that killed Spotlight-style and Notes-style third-party apps gates entry. Android Digital Wellbeing applies the same vector on the Android side.

G5 Why Now INCONCLUSIVE

⚠ Model judgment"AI got better" is the spec's canonical anti-pattern for Why Now. The evidence doesn't surface a sharper thesis (specific API opening, regulatory change, remote-work behavior shift). The absence of evidence is itself the signal.

Verdict math

The fail-2 rule.

Five gate verdicts collapse to one overall verdict by a fixed table. No model discretion at the rollup.

Gate verdicts Overall
5 PASS GO
4 PASS + 1 INCONCLUSIVE CONDITIONAL GO
4 PASS + 1 FAIL CONDITIONAL GO
3 PASS + 2 (FAIL or INCONCLUSIVE) CONDITIONAL GO
2+ FAIL NO-GO

Anti-bias by construction

Four structural defenses.

Confirmation bias is the failure mode of every idea-validation tool. Veto answers it at four points in the pipeline.

  1. Every fact carries a quality tier (S/A/B/C/D) and a bias flag. No fact ships without provenance.
  2. Contradicting-evidence search is mandatory. Every gate fans out a counter-search before the verdict resolves.
  3. Three Validation Checks audit verdicts before they render. Internal consistency, source quality, anti-confirmation.
  4. The Spiky POV section is blank. The framework never decides for the user. The reader writes the take.

5 prompts · 13 tools

Inside the framework.

Prompts are what you call from Claude Desktop. Tools are what runs underneath — the LLM picks them as it works through each gate.

What you call

  • validate_idea Runs the full 5-gate validation on a product idea. Calls every tool the gates need; produces a NO-GO / GO / CONDITIONAL GO verdict with sourced evidence.
  • generate_test_cards Input your business idea, get 3-7 hypotheses that must be true for the business to work — each with a cheap test design, an exact metric, and a pass/fail threshold. Strategyzer Test Card format. Pair with validate_idea to focus tests on the weakest gates.
  • run_single_gate Runs a single gate in isolation. Useful when you want to stress-test just the platform-risk dimension, or just the pricing, without the full report.
  • steelman_against Argues against your own idea. Forces the strongest possible case that this product should not exist.
  • quick_kill_check Five-minute disqualification scan. Cheap, surface-level — catches the obvious kills before you commit to a full validation.

What runs underneath

Gate 1 — Direct Competitor Scan

  • find_closest_competitor Surfaces the closest existing thing in your category — across Web, Product Hunt, and Hacker News.
  • map_competitive_weaknesses Mines Reddit, HN, and the wider web for user complaints. Separates structural weaknesses from surface gripes.
  • read_competitor_changelog Reads a competitor's changelog for failure signals: setup creep, churn language, platform gaps.

Gate 2 — Market Structure

  • estimate_demand_signals Composes GitHub activity, subreddit subscribers, and search-cluster mentions into one demand signal: strong / moderate / weak / none.
  • find_public_revenue_signals Surfaces public revenue evidence — IndieHackers entries, MRR tweets, SEC filings — for your category and its competitors.

Gate 3 — Platform Risk

  • assess_platform_dependency Detects platforms your product sits on top of (App Store, OpenAI, Shopify, etc.) and scores deplatforming risk.
  • check_big_tech_encroachment Scans Apple WWDC, Google I/O, MS Build, Meta Connect, AWS re:Invent for category overlap. The 'will Apple ship this' check.

Gate 4 — Willingness to Pay

  • find_pricing_anchors Reads competitor pricing pages (live + Wayback) and scans G2/Capterra. Auto-flags categories where pricing dropped 25%+ in 24 months.

Gate 5 — Why Now

  • find_why_now_signals Surfaces recent enablers — new APIs, platform shifts, YC RFS touchpoints — that make this idea possible now, not 2 years ago.
  • find_yc_rfs_alignment Scores your idea against YC's current Request for Startups. A signal that smart investors think the category matters.

Cross-gate (synthesis)

  • finalize_validation_report The rollup. Takes the per-gate evidence and produces the final markdown artifact with verdict, killshots, and blank Spiky POV.
  • get_category_failure_modes Searches post-mortems and shutdown stories. Returns the structural failure patterns specific to your category.
  • scan_producthunt_launches Pulls Product Hunt launches in your category. Weighs engagement (comments / votes) to spot high-conviction vs. viral-but-shallow.
